Do You Feel Like a Commodity?

— January 29, 2019 —

Do you feel like a machine or a commodity?

If so, you are not the only one.

According to “The 5 Languages of Appreciation in the Workplace” by Gary Chapman, if no one notices a person’s commitment to doing the job well, that person’s motivation tends to wane over time.

“Next to physical survival, the greatest need of a human being is psychological survival, to be understood, to be affirmed, to be validated, to be appreciated,” notes Steven Covey, author of “The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People.”

So why “Just Say Thanks” doesn’t work? It’s what we are told to repeatedly tell our teams “great job!” and “thanks” especially to millennial workforce population? What researches found is that “just say thanks” can actually backfire, sparking cynicism among the ranks. While we all want to know we are valued, we want it to be authentic, not contrived. Additionally, catching team member doing what you want and saying “thanks” is showing them recognition. This is different from appreciation, which focuses on performance plus team member’s value as a person.

So which appreciation language is yours and can you recognize which appreciation language will hit the mark with your team members? Gary Chapman in his book identifies five appreciation languages including words of affirmation, quality time, tangible gifts, acts of service, and physical touch.

We typically gravitate towards what we value as a sign of appreciation. If you see a team member likes giving presents at work, then that is what they expect in return. Patting that person in the back (physical touch) and saying “I admire how organized your papers are!” (words of affirmation) may completely miss the mark. But giving them a tangible gift (price doesn’t matter!), will make them feel valued and appreciated as person rewarding the organization with a greater employee engagement.

So if you do feel like a commodity, it’s in your hands to tell your leader what is the right way of motivation is for you:

“Leader, I really appreciate you sending me a note of “Thanks.” But what I really would prefer if you showed me your appreciation by giving me some tangible item that will remind me that you know how good of a job I’m doing when I look at it.”

And leaders, I encourage you to look around and see whose face becomes sour instead of happy when you give the words of affirmations that supposed to make it all good, but unfortunately will miss the mark with 50% of your team.
